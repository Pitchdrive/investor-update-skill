# Brightloop — Investor Update, April 2026

*(Fictional company, fictional numbers — this example shows the target structure and tone.)*

## 01 · CEO update

March was our worst sales month since September — €41K against a €55K plan — and I want
to start there rather than with the good news. Two enterprise deals slipped into April;
one has since closed, the other is stalled at security review. The miss was concentration
risk, not demand: our top-3 pipeline deals were 70% of the plan. We're fixing the shape
of the pipeline, not just its size — details in §07.

Last month I committed to three things: ship the self-serve onboarding (done, live
March 18), hire a founding AE (done, starts May 1), and get SOC 2 Type I (missed —
audit moved to April 22, my fault for underestimating the evidence collection).

The good news: activation is working. Self-serve users who finish onboarding convert
to paid at 11% in week one — nearly double our assisted funnel. April's focus is
making that funnel carry half of new revenue.

## 03 · Product

Self-serve onboarding shipped March 18. Time-to-first-value dropped from 3 days
(assisted) to 34 minutes (median, first 120 cohort users). Week-one paid conversion:
11% self-serve vs 6% assisted.

| What changed | Why |
|---|---|
| Onboarding no longer requires a call | The call was our bottleneck, not the user's |
| Templates replace blank-canvas start | 80% of drop-off happened at the blank canvas |

The bet: self-serve becomes the primary funnel and sales becomes expansion-only.
North-stars we'll track publicly in these updates: week-one paid conversion (11%),
median time-to-first-value (34 min), and net revenue retention (currently 104%).
